# Setting
The player takes control over a director of a nuclear shelter as the residents enter and bombs fall outside. The director's task is to manage the shelter, remain in power and shepard the population. There isn't really a defined end and in the mid-to-late game the player can even expand outside but the aim is just to survive.

Game Inspiration:
 - Fallout - General setting, the whole nuclear theme, but probably won't go with 50s USA theme and will go with a different one
 - Frostpunk - Survive and manage in hostile environment, the major thing I want to steal is the factions from Frostpunk 2, in my case I would like to make them dynamic and changing as the shelter situation itself changes, but yeah, these kinds of political blocks putting pressure on the player would be nice
 - CoC/TiTs/Lilith's Throne - The adult theme as well as the movement and interactions of the player character.
 - Freecities - A lot can be stolen from this game as it is sort of similar to my idea, also the detail is unmatched

# Main Ideas

## The Director isn't omnipotent
Before the introduction scene starts the player will pick and choose their starting scenario, situation and their player character. Based on these the shelter can develop in different ways, from an arch that cryogenically freezes majority of it's population to preserve them. To a democratic community to despotic and cut-throat stratified society. All dependent on the Player Character capabilities and the decisions the player takes

## Developed Rule System
While as the Director the player can make decisions and pass orders to restrict and allow certain behaviour the system needs to be more dynamic, allowing delegation to non-player authorities as well formalizing "soft-laws" so to say general traditions different people abide by. 

### Examples
The Director passes order declaring special spaces that neighbor industrial and manufacturing facilities, naming them "Factory Periphery". With another order they declare that the "Factory Periphery" spaces are to be kept clean and littering there is prohibited. the populace accepts the decision and if the player actually wandered the spaces they could see increased cleanliness.

During electricity rationing period factory workers wakeup at 8 to get to work at 9 and end work at 15 when the power shuts-off, establishing a "habit" the members of the factory workers group will follow even after the electricity rationing period ends, unless prompted to change.

## Population Resistance
Every decision causes different levels of friction and each government has it's loyalists and resistance. Different factions and groups have different needs and wants as well as visions for the shelter (more on group ambitions later). Thus, every order passed and every decision made has different influence on the Director's reputation with the different people.

This is meant for two things, first it prompts the player to make counter-productive decisions creating opportunities for interesting gameplay. And the second is the resistance that prevents the player from making too drastic decisions, whatever their aim, after all every political change needs to be gradual otherwise the ruler risks public uprising.

While named resistance this section also includes loyalties. Should the player find groups they ideologically align with or that they could use for their aims earning loyalists is a good idea as they counteract the resistance, support the director and in case of civil war stand on the Director's side.
